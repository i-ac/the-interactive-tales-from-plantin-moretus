Part I : The Stab Incident 1554 - early 1555

In 1554, one fateful night changed everything

Trouble on the Meir Bridge

In 1554, or early 1555, I was attacked on Meir Bridge by a group of drunken men who mistook me for someone else. As I carried a casket to a client, one drew a dagger and stabbed me deeply. It was so deep, he had trouble retrieving back his dagger. Despite the pain, I sat down my basket and I explained the mistake calmly, but the men fled.

“Gentlemen, you are mistaken. What harm have I done you?” 

Half dead or half alive?

Gravely wounded, I was attended to by renowned surgeon Joannes Farinalius, who stitched the deep wound. Though I wasn’t expected to survive the attack, I made it through. But the wound left me physically weakened, and I knew I could no longer continue with bookbinding as before, because it’s a craft that demanded so much strength.

Bookbinding will never be the same again

I need your help now more than ever!
